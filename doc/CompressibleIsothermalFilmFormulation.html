Compressible Isothermal Thin Film Formulation

The compressible Reynolds equation: $$ \begin{equation} \label{eq:1} 12 \mu \frac \partial {\partial t} (ph) + 6 \mu \nabla \centerdot(ph\vec V) - \nabla \centerdot (h^3 p\nabla p) = 0 \end{equation}$$

\(\mu\) is the fluid dynamic viscosity
\(p\) is the pressure field defined on the 2 dimensional lubrication film domain
\(h\) is the film thickness, which may vary over the domain
\(\vec V\) is the relative velocity of the upper and lower bearing surfaces

The weak form of equation \eqref{eq:1} is: $$ \begin{equation} \label{eq:2} (\psi , 12 \mu \frac \partial {\partial t} (ph)) + (\psi,6 \mu \nabla \centerdot(ph\vec V)) - (\psi,\nabla \centerdot (h^3 p\nabla p)) = 0 \end{equation}$$ The divergence theorem (scroll to 'Refresher: The divergence theorem') is applied on the second and third terms of the left hand side of equation \eqref{eq:2} $$ \begin{equation} \label{eq:3} \left(\psi , 12 \mu \frac \partial {\partial t} (ph)\right) -\left(\nabla \psi, 6\mu h p \vec V\right) +\left\langle\psi,6\mu hp\vec V \centerdot \hat n \right\rangle +\left(\nabla \psi , h^3 p \nabla p\right) -\left\langle \psi, h^3 p \nabla p \centerdot \hat n \right\rangle = 0 \end{equation}$$
